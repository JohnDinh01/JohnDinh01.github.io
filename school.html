My Education

After graduating from Clearbrook High School in 2019, I attended San Jacinto Community College for two years to complete my core courses. I then transferred to the University of Houston's Cullen College of Engineering, where I am pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering. I have been at UH for about three years, or six semesters, and during this time, I have learned how to effectively use my resources to my advantage. I've come to realize that simply listening to lectures is not enough for certain tough courses. To truly understand the material, I have found it helpful to form study groups and supplement my learning with additional resources. As a student, it is also important to be involved and take advantage of opportunities to network through events, organizations, and career fairs. Building connections is crucial for success in college and beyond.

I was initially interested in learning about programming and had a passion for math and problem solving, so I decided to pursue engineering. As I researched different engineering disciplines, I became interested in electrical engineering because of its relevance in a digital world that relies heavily on electricity. However, as I learned more about computer engineering, I realized that it combines both computer science and electrical engineering and offers even more opportunities. I decided to switch my major to computer engineering because I believed it was the perfect fit for my interests and goals.
